Medieval Christian Military Orders

A journey into the warrior brotherhoods of the Middle Ages those who fought in the Crusades and saved the Holy land and the East. Click on any order to learn more.

The Poor Fellow-Soldiers of Christ and of the Temple of Solomon

Arguably the most famous military order, the Templars were founded to protect Christian pilgrims in the Holy Land. They became a formidable fighting force and a powerful financial institution before their abrupt and brutal suppression in 1312.

Order of Knights of the Hospital of Saint John of Jerusalem

Beginning as a hospital in Jerusalem to care for sick pilgrims, the Hospitallers evolved into a major military order. After the fall of the Crusader states, they ruled Rhodes and later Malta, famously defending it against the Ottoman Empire in 1565.

Order of Brothers of the German House of Saint Mary in Jerusalem

Formed by German crusaders, the Teutonic Knights aided pilgrims and established hospitals. They are most famous for their campaigns in the Baltic region, where they established their own monastic state in Prussia and fought against pagan Lithuanians and Orthodox Russians.

Order of Saint James of the Sword

A Spanish order founded to protect pilgrims on the way to the shrine of St. James in Compostela and to fight the Moors during the Reconquista. Their symbol is a distinctive red cross fleury, resembling a sword.

Order of Calatrava

The first military order founded in Castile, Spain. It was established by Cistercian monks who took up arms to defend the fortress of Calatrava against the Moors. They played a crucial role in the Reconquista of the Iberian Peninsula.

Order of Alcántara

Another key Spanish military order, founded in the Kingdom of León. Initially known as the Knights of San Julián del Pereiro, they were entrusted with the defense of the town of Alcántara and adopted its name.

Order of Saint Benedict of Aviz

A Portuguese military order that followed the Cistercian rule. They played a vital role in the Portuguese Reconquista and were instrumental in the establishment of the Aviz dynasty which ruled Portugal for nearly two centuries.

Military Order of Christ

The Portuguese successor to the Knights Templar. Following the suppression of the Templars, King Dinis I of Portugal transferred their properties and legacy to this new order, which later became a key funder of Portugal's Age of Discovery.

Fratres militiae Christi de Livonia

A German military order established to conquer and convert the pagan peoples of Livonia (modern-day Latvia and Estonia). After a devastating defeat in 1236, the remaining members were incorporated into the Teutonic Knights as an autonomous branch.

Fratres Milites Christi de Dobrin

A small military order created in Poland to provide protection against the incursions of pagan Prussians. They were eventually absorbed by the Teutonic Order, though some knights resisted the merger and joined the Templars or Hospitallers instead.

Military and Hospitaller Order of Saint Lazarus of Jerusalem

Originally a hospitaller order caring for those with leprosy, it is believed to have taken on military duties during the 12th century. Knights with leprosy from other orders often joined the Order of Saint Lazarus.

Order of Our Lady of Montesa

Founded in the Kingdom of Aragon (Spain) to take over the assets of the suppressed Knights Templar and continue the fight against the Moors. It was established with the approval of the Pope and named after its main fortress.

Order of the Holy Sepulchre

Established to protect the Church of the Holy Sepulchre in Jerusalem. While primarily a chivalric and religious institution, it had military functions during the Crusades. Today it is a Papal Order of Chivalry.

Hospitallers of St Thomas of Canterbury at Acre

An English order founded during the Third Crusade in Acre. It was created to tend to the sick and bury the dead, particularly those of English origin, and later took on military responsibilities.

Order of Saint Mary of Spain

A short-lived Castilian naval order founded by King Alfonso X to aid in the Reconquista at sea. After several major naval defeats, its remnants were merged into the Order of Santiago.

Knights of the Cross with the Red Star

A hospitaller order founded in Bohemia (modern-day Czech Republic). Their primary mission was to care for the sick and poor, as well as to guard the bridges they built, which had a military function.

Order of Our Lady of Bethlehem

A military order founded by Pope Pius II to defend the island of Lemnos against the Ottoman Turks. It was poorly funded and short-lived, dissolving after only a few years following the Ottoman reconquest of the island.
